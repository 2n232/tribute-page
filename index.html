Gral. San Martin

The man who freed three countries

Here's a time line of General San Martin

  • 1778:Born in Yapeyu on February 25th, he's the youngest of five siblings, his father was Don Juan de San Martin and his mother Gregoria Matorras.

  • 1783: Martín's family move to Spain.

  • 1789: He enter as a cadet in the Murcia regiment of Spain.

  • 1791: He participatedfor the first time in combat in Oran, north of Africa.

  • 1796: His father died in Malaga

  • 1804: San Martín stands out for his bravery and courage in Cádiz.

  • 1808: He join the Bourbon Cavalry Regiment and was appointed Lieutenant Colonel.

  • 1810: He decide to return home

  • 1811: San Martin asks for his retirement from the spanish army and goes to Buenos Aires

  • 1812: On March 16, the Triumvirate granted him the rank of Lieutenant Colonel of Cavalry. He creates the Horse Grenadier Regiment. Together with Carlos María de Alvear, he founded the Lautaro Lodge, a secret organization that proposed to fight for the freedom of America. She marries Remedios de Escalada. She participates in the overthrow of the First Triumvirate, considering it unpopular and centralist. This will face him for life with Rivadavia.

  • 1813: On February 3, the victory of San Lorenzo takes place, baptism of fire for the grenadiers. On December 3, he was appointed Chief of the Army of the North, replacing General Belgrano, defeated at Vilcapugio and Ayohuma. On March 28, his mother, Gregoria Matorras, died in Spain.

  • 1814: San Martín meets with Belgrano at the Posta de Yatasto (Salta) and takes charge of the Army of the North. He concentrated the troops in Tucumán and entrusted the defense of the northern border to Martín Miguel de Güemes, whom he named Lieutenant Colonel. In May his health deteriorated and he retired to Córdoba in the company of his friend Tomás Guido to recover and prepare his continental plan: to liberate Chile and from there to embark for Peru and put an end to royalist power in Lima (Peru). San Martín is appointed governor of Cuyo

  • 1816: San Martín governs Cuyo promoting education, industry and agriculture. He creates an equitable tax system so that those who have more will pay more. He is appointed General in Chief of the Army of the Andes. On August 24, Mercedes Tomasa de San Martín y Escalada was born in Mendoza. The Army of the Andes settles in the Plumerillo military camp.

  • 1817:On January 18, the Army of the Andes begins the crossing of the mountain range. On February 12, San Martín and his army, already in Chile, defeated the royalists in Chacabuco. Two days later San Martín enters Santiago. On March 18, he left for Buenos Aires in search of help to continue his campaign.

  • 1818: On February 12, the Independence of Chile is proclaimed in Santiago. The defeat suffered at Cancha Rayada is widely surpassed by the triumph of Maipú on April 5, which consolidates Chilean independence.

  • 1819: San Martín disobeys the order of the Directory to abandon his liberation campaign to repress the federales of the Litoral.

  • 1820: San Martín resigns from his position as head of the Army of the Andes, but is confirmed by his own companions with the signing of the Act of Rancagua. He is designated general in chief of the Expedition to Peru. On A ugust 20, he set sail with the liberating fleet for Lima. On September 12, San Martín disembarks in the Peruvian port of Pisco.

  • 1821: On July 12, he entered Lima with his troops and proclaimed the Independence of Peru. He is designated Protector of Peru. On August 28 he abolishes slavery and the mita and creates the Library of Peru. In Buenos Aires, Martín Rodríguez governs with Rivadavia as Minister of Government

  • 1822: Bolívar incorpora Ecuador a la Gran Colombia. El 26 y 27 de julio se entrevistan los dos libertadores en Guayaquil. San Martín deja a Bolívar la conducción de la guerra y vuelve a Lima. Renuncia a todos los cargos y se dirige a Chile. Los EE.UU. reconocen la independencia argentina.

  • 1823: Deja Chile y se traslada a Mendoza instalándose en su chacra de Los Barriales. El gobierno porteño, a través de Rivadavia, le niega el permiso para trasladarse a Buenos Aires y encontrarse con su familia. El 3 de agosto muere su esposa, Remedios de Escalada, a los 25 años. El 4 de diciembre San Martín llega a Buenos Aires.

  • 1824: On February 10, he embarks for Europe with his daughter Mercedes. On April 23, she arrives in France where she is denied a visa. She must continue her journey to England.

  • 1825: He writes the maxims for his daughter who was studying in London at the time.

  • 1829: On February 6, San Martín arrived in Buenos Aires and upon learning of Dorrego's execution, he decided not to disembark and settled for three months in Montevideo. On December 8, Rosas is appointed Governor of Buenos Aires.

  • 1830: San Martín and his daughter settle in France.

  • 1838: France blocks the port of Buenos Aires. On August 5, in a letter, San Martín offers Rosas his military services. Rosas thanks him for the gesture and asks him to make diplomatic efforts.

  • 1839: Rosas names San Martín Plenipotentiary Minister of the Argentine Confederation before the Republic of Peru.

  • 1850: On August 17, he died at his home at 3:00 p.m.

  • 1880: President Nicolás Avellaneda manages to transfer his remains to the Cathedral of Buenos Aires.
